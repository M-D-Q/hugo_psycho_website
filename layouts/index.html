Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a highly effective treatment that focuses on changing patterns of thinking or behavior that are behind people's difficulties, and so changing the way they feel. It is used to treat a wide range of issues in a person's life, from sleeping difficulties or relationship problems, to drug and alcohol abuse or anxiety and depression. CBT works by changing people's attitudes and their behavior by focusing on the thoughts, images, beliefs and attitudes that are held (a person's cognitive processes) and how these processes relate to the way a person behaves, as a way of dealing with emotional problems.

Gestalt Therapy

Gestalt Therapy emphasizes personal responsibility and focuses on the individual's experience in the present moment, the therapist-client relationship, the environmental and social contexts of a person's life, and the self-regulating adjustments people make as a result of their overall situation. It integrates the body and mind factors, allowing clients to become more self-aware and to understand the blockages that may hinder their personal growth and development.

Art Therapy

Art Therapy is a form of psychotherapy that uses art media as its primary mode of communication. It is based on the belief that the creative process involved in artistic self-expression helps people to resolve conflicts and problems, develop interpersonal skills, manage behavior, reduce stress, increase self-esteem and self-awareness, and achieve insight. Art therapy integrates psychotherapeutic techniques with the creative process to improve mental health and well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

This section contains answers to commonly asked questions about our psychotherapy services.

Individual psychotherapy involves one-on-one sessions with a therapist, focusing on your personal issues and concerns. It can help you understand your feelings, thoughts, and behaviors, providing strategies to cope with specific challenges, improve relationships, and enhance overall well-being.

Each therapy session typically lasts between 45 to 60 minutes. The length can vary based on the type of therapy and the individual's needs.

In your first session, the therapist will typically discuss your personal history, the reasons you're seeking therapy, and your goals for treatment. It's a chance for you to get to know your therapist and decide if their approach feels right for you.

Yes, all discussions in therapy are confidential and protected by client-therapist privilege. There are some exceptions, such as when there's a risk of harm to yourself or others, which your therapist will explain in your first session.

Considering therapy can be a step if you're feeling overwhelmed, facing persistent sadness, anxiety, or if you're dealing with significant life changes or challenges. Therapy can provide support, strategies for coping, and a space to explore your thoughts and feelings.

Yes, we offer online therapy sessions to provide flexibility and convenience. These sessions are conducted via secure video conferencing platforms, ensuring confidentiality and accessibility.
